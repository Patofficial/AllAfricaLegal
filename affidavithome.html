About Affidavits

An Affidavit is the legal way to swear that your statements are fact. You'll sign an Affidavit document in front of a commissioner for oaths or margistrate to finalize it. If you've been asked for an Affidavit, you're being trusted to tell the whole truth. Making a flase statement in an affidavit is punishable by imprisonment for a term of up to two years!!!

Use an Affidavit if:-

  • You have been asked to make a declaration or statement of fact under oath as part of a contract or legal process.
  • You want to ask someone else to make a declaration or statement of fact under oath.

Common types of Affidavits

  • Declaration of Customary Marriage commonly used to notify authorities of marriage conducted under tribal customs and rituals.
  • Declaration of name If you go by a name different than your birth name but haven't gotten around to officially changing it, use a declaration of name affidavit to notify others of the various names.
  • Declaration of lost documents In the event you lose original documents and need to declare that they are lost in order to get replacements.
  • Declaration of financial support This affidavit form simply states that the signer will be financially responsible for the "dependant".
  • Declaration of dependants Commonly used to notify authorities of your dependants.
  • Declaration of seperation In the event that you have separated from your spouse and wish to notify the authorities.
  • General Affidavit If you can't find exactly the affidavit you need, in most cases, a general affidavit form can be used.
